The big event on the horizon is the much-anticipated garlic harvest! Wet soil has delayed us, but drier conditions this week should allow.

We continue to replant our warm-weather crops such as lettuces and other greens. It is also time to start cool-season crops like kohlrabi and turnips.
Tending our tomatoes is ongoing, with retying a necessity as the plants grow. Please see the Prudent Pruning item for tips on removing sideshoots or suckers. With July now over, we should drastically slow-down harvesting of our rhubarb to allow the root system to regenerate for next year's crop.

 Washer in hose coupling. For all of us working in the garden, here's something to be aware of: The tap-end of the black hose coupling should always have a washer seated inside. Without it, a full seal is impossible. If you are connecting or disconnecting the hose from the tap, kindly inspect the coupling. If the washer is not fully seated, please push it down into place.
